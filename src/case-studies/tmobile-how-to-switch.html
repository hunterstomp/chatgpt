T-Mobile Purchase Simplicity: How to Switch

Designing a Seamless Switching Experience: Leveraging UX, Chatbots, and Omnichannel Integration for New Customers.

Client
T-Mobile
Role
Senior UX Designer
Duration
8 Months
Team
6 Designers, Product, Engineering

Project Summary

Problem

Prospects struggled to understand the steps, eligibility, and total cost of switching. Drop‑off spiked during plan compare, promo eligibility, and device trade‑in.

Approach

Mapped the end‑to‑end journey; prototyped a chat‑first guided flow with eligibility checker, plan/line estimator, and device trade‑in path.

Result

Clearer switching guidance and fewer dead‑ends; faster completion time in prototypes; stronger chat→store continuity.

Chat Animation Insights

Design Considerations

Many explorations were conducted for chat animation designs for both desktop and mobile, showing how a chat notification could appear, fade, collapse into a circle, and initiate a full chat window.

  • Detailed specifications for timing and easing
  • User-triggered interaction to avoid annoyance
  • Professional appearance to avoid "teenage" perception
  • Seamless integration with existing interface

Key Learning

Some participants found the chat bubble or help emoji on T-Mobile.com annoying and distracting, reinforcing a "teenage" side of T-Mobile that might deter professionals. This suggests a need for careful implementation and user-triggered interaction.

Store Locator Insights

No Rug Pulling

Our primary concern was to not pull the rug out for Sprint customers during and after the acquisition. But also not to confuse T-Mobile customers with new and gaudy branding.

  • Clear, direct CTAs to initiate switching process online
  • Store locator integration alongside online experience
  • Seamless journey regardless of customer's preferred channel
  • Maintained brand consistency during transition

Strategic Approach

This indicates that clear, direct CTAs to initiate the switching process online are needed alongside the store locator, ensuring a seamless journey regardless of the customer's preferred channel.

Lessons Learned

User-Centered Design Wins

The success of Design J (Jump Links) in usability testing reinforced the importance of user-centered design. Users preferred the jump link design for its ability to provide easy access to information and enable in-line checks, such as number portability and coverage.

Professional Perception Matters

The feedback about chat bubbles being "annoying and distracting" highlighted the importance of maintaining a professional appearance. This was particularly crucial for business users who might be deterred by overly casual or "teenage" design elements.

Omnichannel Integration is Critical

The project underscored the importance of seamless integration between online and offline channels. Clear CTAs and store locator integration ensured customers could complete their journey regardless of their preferred channel.

Customer Service Communication

The project highlighted the opportunity to communicate T-Mobile's "phenomenal customer service" more directly, as this was a known strength that wasn't always evident online. This became a key focus area for future improvements.
