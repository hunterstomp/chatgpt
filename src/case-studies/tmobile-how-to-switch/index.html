Project Summary
  • Problem — Prospects struggled to understand the steps, eligibility, and total cost of switching. Drop‑off spiked during plan compare, promo eligibility, and device trade‑in; chat, web, and store handoffs were disjointed.
  • Approach — Mapped the end‑to‑end journey; prototyped a chat‑first guided flow with an eligibility checker, plan/line estimator, and device trade‑in path. Carried context into store appointments and support. Ran usability tests to simplify copy and reduce decision load.
  • Result — Clearer switching guidance and fewer dead‑ends; faster completion time in prototypes; stronger chat→store continuity.
  1. Customer Journey Mapping. This was a primary research method, used extensively to outline customer behaviours and attitudes throughout the product lifecycle. I led journey mapping workshops with internal teams and subject matter experts (SMEs), including health professionals, to explore, test, and validate new industry verticals.
  2. Discovery.  Working with SMEs we covered the top problems that plague America:  Atrial Fibrillation, Hypertension, and Diabetes, outlining present and future states of care, incorporating wearable technology and triage tools. 
  3. Additional Design. Prospect and base customer journeys were also mapped for Apple Business Chat (ABC), from seeing an ad to picking up a phone and ongoing support

Project Kickoff

Problem

Prospects struggled to understand the steps, eligibility, and total cost of switching. Drop‑off spiked during plan compare, promo eligibility, and device trade‑in; chat, web, and store handoffs were disjointed.

Goal

The project aimed to increase conversion among prospects intimidated by the process by demystifying it and providing tools and clear guidance for switching in any chosen channel.

This effort also sought to improve T-Mobile's online presence, which was perceived as highly optimised for selling phones and technology, but lacking in showcasing brand values of diversity and customer obsession. Consumers often noted a lack of human element, smiles, or explicit mentions of customer service and happiness on the website.
Specific customer pain points identified through research included issues with customer loyalty, rising prices, unreliable coverage/reception, slow data speeds, confusing billing, and a perception that new customers received better offers. Additionally, there was a challenge in integrating new health and wellness services with telecommunications capabilities.
